Paneer Butter Masala

“Rich. Creamy. Comfort in every bite.”

Serves: 3-4 people

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Rest Time: 0 minutes

Cooking Time: 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 10-12 cashews (soaked)
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish)
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Soak cashews in warm water for 15 minutes and blend to a smooth paste.
  2. Heat butter and oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until raw smell disappears.
  5. Stir in tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  6. Add turmeric, chili powder, salt, and cashew paste. Cook for 5–7 minutes.
  7. Add paneer cubes and 1/2 cup water. Mix gently and simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Stir in cream and garam masala. Simmer for 2 more minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with naan or rice.

FAQs:

Q: Can I fry the paneer before adding?
A: Yes! Lightly frying paneer in oil or ghee gives it a firmer texture and richer taste.

Q: Is it okay to skip cashews?
A: You can substitute with cream or melon seeds for a creamy base, or skip for a lighter version.

Q: Can I make this vegan?
A: Use tofu instead of paneer, plant-based butter/oil, and coconut cream or cashew cream.

Q: How long can it be stored?
A: Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Reheat gently before serving.
